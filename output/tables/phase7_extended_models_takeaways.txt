Phase 7 Extended Models - Capstone Takeaways

Core meaning:
Phase 7 strengthens the main capstone claim that MIDAS outperforms ARIMAX
because it preserves economically meaningful weekly timing information, not
because it simply uses more predictors, compresses data better, or overfits.

1. Directional and large-move performance
- ARIMAX RMSE: 0.02986; directional accuracy: 64.6%.
- MIDAS nbeta RMSE: 0.02057 (-31.1% vs ARIMAX), best RMSE model.
- MIDAS nealmon RMSE: 0.02104 (-29.5% vs ARIMAX), best directional accuracy at 80.2%.
- MIDAS nealmon, MIDAS nbeta, U-MIDAS, and CLM-SS (12 lags) all hit 100% of large up-moves and large down-moves.
- ARIMAX misses half of the large down-moves.

2. LASSO-MIDAS
- LASSO-MIDAS RMSE: 0.02103 (-29.6% vs ARIMAX), almost identical to MIDAS nealmon.
- Directional accuracy: 76.0%.
- Best lambda: 0.000721.
- 11 of 12 weekly lags survive; only wti_m2_w1 is zeroed out.
- Largest coefficients are wti_m1_w2 (0.1848) and wti_m1_w3 (0.1570).
- Interpretation: LASSO independently confirms the prior-month weeks 2-3 lag hump found by MIDAS, CLM-SS, and XGBoost.

3. XGBoost
- XGBoost RMSE: 0.02365 (-20.8% vs ARIMAX).
- Directional accuracy: 72.9%.
- XGBoost beats ARIMAX but does not beat MIDAS.
- Important features include prior-month WTI weeks 2-3, confirming the same 5-7 week transmission window from a tree-based ML model.

4. Forecast horizon validity
- h=1: MIDAS nbeta RMSE 0.02057 (-31.1% vs ARIMAX), statistically significant DM p=0.0005.
- h=2: MIDAS nbeta RMSE 0.02419 (-14.9% vs ARIMAX), still useful, DM p=0.0340.
- h=3: MIDAS nbeta RMSE 0.03023 (+2.2% vs ARIMAX), no MIDAS advantage.
- Interpretation: the capstone should describe the main empirical exercise as one-month nowcasting. MIDAS is strongest at h=1, still useful at h=2, and fades by h=3.

5. Year-by-year stress test
- MIDAS-type models beat ARIMAX in most years.
- XGBoost performs especially well in 2018 and 2020.
- U-MIDAS wins in 2022.
- ARIMAX is never the yearly RMSE winner among the reported models.
- Interpretation: MIDAS advantage is not only a COVID artifact; the gains appear across normal and crisis periods, though the best model varies by regime.

6. PCA + VIF
- Max pairwise absolute correlation among the 12 weekly WTI lags: 0.254.
- Median VIF: 1.11; max VIF: 1.25.
- There is no multicollinearity concern.
- PCA needs 11 components to explain 95% of variance.
- First 4 PCs explain only 47.85% of variance.
- PCA-ARIMAX RMSE: 0.02859 (-4.3% vs ARIMAX), far worse than MIDAS nbeta (-31.1%).
- Interpretation: PCA compression alone does not solve the mixed-frequency problem. MIDAS wins because it preserves weekly lag timing and estimates interpretable weights.

Main thesis paragraph:
The extended diagnostics show that the MIDAS advantage is robust and interpretable. LASSO-MIDAS, XGBoost, CLM-SS, and parametric MIDAS all identify prior-month weeks 2-3 as the most predictive WTI observations for monthly CPI Energy, implying a 5-7 week oil-to-consumer-price transmission window. PCA and VIF diagnostics show that the result is not driven by multicollinearity or generic dimensionality reduction: the weekly lag matrix has very low VIF values, PCA-ARIMAX improves ARIMAX only marginally, and MIDAS remains far superior. Forecast-horizon analysis further shows that the result is strongest as a one-month nowcasting exercise, remains useful at h=2, and fades by h=3.
